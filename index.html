MEET THE REPTILES


Reptiles are a diverse group of cold-blooded vertebrates that have existed on Earth for millions of years. From slithering snakes and armored crocodiles to graceful sea turtles, they are found in a wide variety of environments across the globe, thriving on land, in water, and in between.


Reptiles are ectothermic, meaning they rely on external sources of heat to regulate their body temperature. They are recognized for their dry, scaly skin, which helps protect them and reduce water loss. Most reptiles lay eggs, although some species give birth to live young. Unlike amphibians, reptiles breathe entirely through lungs.

Why Are Reptiles Important?

Reptiles play a vital role in maintaining ecological balance. They help control insect and rodent populations, serve as both predators and prey, and contribute to the health of ecosystems. Their presence is an important indicator of environmental stability and biodiversity.
